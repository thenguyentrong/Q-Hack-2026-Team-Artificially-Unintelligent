# DESIGN.md: Agnes Procurement Intelligence — Q-Hack 2026 ## 1. Project Vision **Agnes** is a modular decision-support system designed to transform procurement from a logistical task into a data-driven intelligence workflow. By leveraging the Spherecast architecture, Agnes identifies consolidation opportunities and automates the verification of technical raw materials using traceable evidence. ### Core Value Proposition - **Molecular-Level Resilience:** Moving beyond vendor lists to understand the chemical and technical "fingerprint" of materials. - **Glass-Box Explainability:** Every recommendation is backed by a visible reasoning path and document-linked evidence. - **Strategic Optimization:** Balancing cost savings with supply chain resilience and Scope 3 sustainability targets. --- ## 2. The 4-Step Decision Pipeline The application is structured as a linear, minimalist process chain to guide users through complex material substitutions. ### Step 1: Selection & Discovery - **User Action:** Selects a finished product (e.g., Gold Standard Whey) and drills down into the Bill of Materials (BOM). - **System Action:** Identifies high-risk or high-value components (e.g., Whey Protein Isolate) as candidates for substitution. - **Key UI:** `Step 1: Product & BOM Selection` — Focuses on visibility of component attributes like SKU, lead time, and current unit cost. ### Step 2: Requirements & Constraints - **User Action:** Review and refine the "Hard" and "Soft" constraints that define a successful substitution. - **System Action:** Agnes programmatically pulls internal brand claims (e.g., "Grass-Fed") and external regulatory data (e.g., EU Food Law, Informed Sport standards). - **Key UI:** `Step 2: Requirements & Constraints` — A unified catalog of constraints including Purity Profiles, Certifications, and Ethical Sourcing. ### Step 3: Verification & Assessment - **User Action:** Compare top supplier candidates side-by-side. - **System Action:** Automatically scans supplier technical data sheets (TDS) and certificates of analysis (COA) to verify compliance. - **Key UI:** `Step 3: Verification Matrix` — Features a detailed technical comparison (Purity, Mineral Profile, Processing Method) and an Expanded Evidence Repository. ### Step 4: Final Recommendation - **User Action:** Review the AI-generated rationale and execute the decision. - **System Action:** Synthesizes cost, risk, and sustainability data into a clear "Top Pick" with an explainable executive summary. - **Key UI:** `Step 4: Final Recommendation` — Provides the "Reasoning Path" (e.g., "15% logistics savings offset by 4% unit price increase") and the final "Execute Decision" trigger. --- ## 3. Design System: Metric Cobalt The UI utilizes the **Metric Cobalt** design system to project a professional, technical, and data-centric aesthetic. - **Typography:** Manrope (Display) for clarity and Inter (Body) for technical data density. - **Color Palette:** Deep navy (#1B263B) for authority, accented by precision greens and reds for pass/fail verification status. - **Component Strategy:** Minimalist containers, flat elevations, and a global process pipeline at the top of every screen to reduce cognitive load during complex analysis. --- ## 4. Technical Logic & Data Strategy - **Layer 0-4 Modularization:** Each step corresponds to a standalone logic layer defined in the Agnes PoC spec. - **Evidence Trail:** Direct linking between verification results and source PDF documents in the Evidence Repository. - **AI Reasoning:** The use of LLMs to infer "Inferred Strict Requirements" from existing product certifications (e.g., if a product is NSF certified, the substitute *must* be as well). --- *Prepared for Q-Hack 2026 by Team Artificially Unintelligent.*
